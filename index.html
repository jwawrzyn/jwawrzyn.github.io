About Me

Professional

Allow me to introduce myself—my name is Jenny Wawrzynczak. With over 15 years of experience as a software developer, I've had the privilege of immersing myself in the world of coding and technology. But there's more to me than just lines of code and algorithms.

From a young age, I discovered a deep passion for golf, a sport that has brought me immeasurable joy throughout my life. Little did I know that my journey as a software developer and my love for golf would one day intertwine in the most fortuitous way imaginable.

Today, I find myself in an incredibly fortunate position, where these two worlds collide harmoniously. I am humbled to write software for a golf non-profit organization, a role that allows me to combine my technical expertise with my unwavering passion for the game.

Having dedicated countless hours to mastering the craft of software development, I have honed my skills to deliver innovative solutions and push the boundaries of what is possible. But it is my deep-rooted love for golf that adds an extra dimension to my work, infusing it with purpose and meaning.

Every line of code I write carries the potential to empower this golf non-profit organization, enabling them to reach new heights and enhance the experiences of golfers around the world. It is an incredible privilege to contribute to the growth and impact of the sport I hold so dear.

My journey stands as a testament to the remarkable synergy that can be found when one's personal and professional passions align. The convergence of my software development expertise and my lifelong devotion to golf has brought me profound fulfillment and the opportunity to make a lasting difference.

I invite you to join me on this extraordinary adventure as I continue to write the next chapter of my story—an exceptional software developer driven by a deep love for golf, poised to create meaningful change in the world of technology and the golfing community alike.

Raspberry Pi Wireless Photo Frame

I got the idea for this from http://www.ofbrooklyn.com/2014/01/2/building-photo-frame-raspberry-pi-motion-detector/. I thought that it would be really awesome and a great way to get into a Raspberry Pi project and I love showing off my photos. I took what he did and got my raspberry pi up and running, tested out a sensor and used some of his code and modified some to deal with flickr authentication due to the fact that my photos are mostly private.

I also just updated it to use play nicer with flickr's update to SSL.

You can check out the code at: https://github.com/jwawrzyn/raspberrypiframe
